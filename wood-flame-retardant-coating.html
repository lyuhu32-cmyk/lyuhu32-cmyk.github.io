What Is Flame Retardant Coating for Wood?

Flame retardant coating for wood is a specialized fire protection material designed to reduce the spread of flames on timber surfaces. When exposed to fire, the coating forms a protective barrier that slows down combustion and minimizes fire damage.

Key Features of Our Fire Retardant Paint for Timber

  • Effective flame spread reduction
  • Water-based environmentally friendly formula
  • Strong adhesion to plywood and solid wood
  • Transparent or white finish options
  • Suitable for interior decorative wood protection

Technical Specifications

  • Fire Rating: B1 / Class A2 (depending on system)
  • Coverage: 0.2–0.3 kg/m² per coat
  • Drying Time: 4–6 hours (surface dry)
  • Application Method: Spray / Brush / Roller
  • Recommended Coats: 2–3 layers

Application Areas

  • Wooden ceilings and panels
  • Interior decorative timber
  • Stage and theater sets
  • Schools and public buildings
  • Hotels and commercial interiors

Frequently Asked Questions

Does flame retardant coating make wood completely fireproof?

No coating can make wood completely fireproof. However, it significantly reduces flame spread and improves fire safety performance.

Is the coating suitable for outdoor use?

This product is mainly designed for interior applications. For outdoor exposure, a protective topcoat is recommended.

Can it maintain the natural wood appearance?

Yes. Transparent versions are available to preserve the natural texture of timber surfaces.
