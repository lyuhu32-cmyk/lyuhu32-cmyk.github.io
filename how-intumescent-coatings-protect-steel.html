How Intumescent Coatings Protect Structural Steel in Fire

Technical Guide by Fire Protection Manufacturer

Why Steel Needs Fire Protection

Structural steel is widely used in modern construction due to its strength and flexibility. However, steel rapidly loses strength when exposed to high temperatures. At approximately 500°C–600°C, steel can lose up to 50% of its load-bearing capacity.

Without proper fire protection, structural failure can occur during fire incidents. This is why fireproof coating systems are essential in commercial and industrial buildings.

What Is an Intumescent Coating?

An intumescent coating is a reactive fire protection material applied to steel surfaces. When exposed to fire, it expands significantly and forms a thick insulating char layer.

This expansion can increase coating thickness multiple times, creating a thermal barrier that slows heat transfer to the steel substrate.

How the Expansion Process Works

When temperatures rise, the coating undergoes a chemical reaction:

  • Acid source activates
  • Carbon source forms protective char
  • Blowing agent creates expansion

The result is a stable insulating layer that protects structural steel from rapid temperature increase.

Fire Resistance Ratings

Depending on coating thickness and steel section factor, intumescent coatings can provide fire resistance ratings ranging from 30 minutes to 120 minutes.

Proper thickness calculation is critical to achieving required building code compliance.

Advantages of Intumescent Steel Fireproof Coatings

  • Lightweight compared to traditional fireproof boards
  • Smooth architectural finish
  • Compatible with decorative topcoats
  • Suitable for exposed steel structures
  • Efficient application by spray systems
