What Is Steel Fireproof Coating?

Steel fireproof coating, also known as intumescent paint for steel, is a fire-resistant coating designed to protect structural steel during fire exposure. When exposed to high temperatures, the coating expands and forms a thick insulating char layer that delays steel temperature rise and prevents structural collapse.

Key Features of Our Intumescent Paint for Steel

  • High expansion ratio under fire exposure
  • Excellent adhesion to structural steel
  • Water-based and low VOC formula
  • Long-term durability and weather resistance
  • Suitable for indoor and semi-exposed environments

Technical Specifications

  • Fire Resistance Rating: 1–3 Hours
  • Dry Film Thickness: 0.8–3.5 mm
  • Density: 1.2 ± 0.1 g/cm³
  • Application Method: Spray / Brush / Roller
  • Surface Preparation: Sa2.5 Sandblasted Steel

Frequently Asked Questions

How does intumescent paint protect steel?

When exposed to fire, the coating expands up to 20–30 times its original thickness, creating an insulating carbon layer that slows heat transfer to the steel substrate.

What fire resistance rating can it achieve?

Depending on the dry film thickness, our coating system can provide 1 to 3 hours of certified fire protection.

Is it suitable for outdoor use?

For fully exposed outdoor environments, a compatible topcoat is recommended to enhance weather resistance.
