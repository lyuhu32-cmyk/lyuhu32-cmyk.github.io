Fire Resistance Rating for Structural Steel

Understanding 30, 60, 90 & 120 Minute Fire Protection Systems

What Is a Fire Resistance Rating?

A fire resistance rating indicates how long a structural element can withstand fire exposure while maintaining structural integrity, insulation performance and load-bearing capacity.

For structural steel, fire resistance ratings are commonly expressed in minutes, such as 30, 60, 90 or 120 minutes.

Common Fire Resistance Rating Levels

  • 30 Minutes: Basic fire protection for low-risk structures
  • 60 Minutes: Standard protection for many commercial buildings
  • 90 Minutes: Enhanced fire resistance for public facilities
  • 120 Minutes: High-level protection for critical infrastructure

Why Structural Steel Requires Fire Protection

Although steel is non-combustible, it rapidly loses strength at high temperatures. At approximately 550°C, steel can lose nearly half of its structural capacity.

Without fire protection coating systems, structural steel elements may deform or fail during prolonged fire exposure.

How Intumescent Coatings Achieve Fire Ratings

Intumescent fireproof coatings expand when exposed to high temperatures, forming an insulating char layer. This barrier slows heat transfer to the steel substrate.

By controlling coating thickness based on section factor and critical temperature, different fire resistance ratings can be achieved.

Fire Rating Calculation Factors

  • Steel section factor (Hp/A)
  • Critical steel temperature
  • Required fire duration
  • Coating dry film thickness
  • Building code requirements
